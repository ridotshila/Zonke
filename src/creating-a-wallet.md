# Creating a Wallet

To access the Zonke platform by Coxygen Global, every user must first create a secure digital wallet. This wallet serves as your personal account for logging in, managing activities, and interacting with the system.

## Getting Started

### 🆕 Step 1: Click "New"

On the main interface, click the "New" button to begin the wallet creation process. This will open the registration form.

### 📧 Step 2: Enter Your Email Address

Provide your primary email address.

**Requirements:**
- The email must be valid and active
- You must have access to it

**Why this matters:**
- It identifies your wallet
- It may be used for notifications or recovery

### 🔐 Step 3: System-Generated PIN Code

After entering your email, the system will automatically generate a secure PIN code for your wallet.

**Purpose of auto-generation:**
- Prevents weak PINs (e.g., 1234, 0000)
- Avoids duplicate PINs between users
- Ensures a consistent level of security

> ⚠️ **Important:** Users cannot create their own PIN. This is intentional to protect accounts.

### 🔁 Step 4: Confirm Your PIN

You will be required to re-enter the generated PIN.

This step ensures:
- You have correctly saved the PIN
- There are no typing errors

### ✅ Step 5: Create Wallet

Click the "Create Wallet" button to complete the process.

Once successful, your wallet will be created and ready for use.

## What Happens Next?

After creating your wallet:
- You can proceed to log in
- Your email becomes your unique identifier
- Your PIN will be required for authentication

## 🔒 Security Guidelines

To keep your wallet safe:
- Keep your PIN confidential
- Do not share your credentials with anyone
- Avoid storing your PIN in unsecured locations
- Always double-check your email before submission

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

Avoid the following issues:
- Entering an incorrect or inaccessible email
- Forgetting to save the generated PIN
- Entering the wrong PIN during confirmation
- Attempting to reuse someone else's credentials

> 💡 **Helpful Tip:** It is recommended to store your PIN in a secure location, such as:
> - A trusted password manager
> - A private offline note
